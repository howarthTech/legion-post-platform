Questions posts ask

Straight answers.

Does the post own its website and content?
Yes. The domain is registered in your post's name and every word and photo stays yours. If you ever leave, you take your domain, your content, and your member list with you.
We already have a site with another service. Is switching painful?
No — we do the move. We rebuild your content on the new site, and we set up permanent redirects so every old link (Facebook posts, flyers, search results) lands on the right new page. Your old site stays up until the new one is live.
Who updates the site when officers or events change?
You email us the change and we post it. There's no software for your officers to learn and no password to lose track of. Posts that want to publish events themselves can ask us about direct access.
Is the texting service legal?
It's designed around the FCC's rules (TCPA) from the ground up: members must text YES before they get anything, every message includes opt-out instructions, STOP is honored automatically, reminders only send during daytime hours, and every message in and out is logged so consent can always be shown.
What does this cost the post, all-in?
$149 a year for the website, $50 a year more if you add SMS reminders. Your domain registration (usually about $20 a year, paid to your registrar) stays in the post's name. That's the whole bill.
